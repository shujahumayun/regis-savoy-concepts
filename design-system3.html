The rendered companion to design3.md. Built from scratch for Concept 2 — sans-serif only, no gold, ink-graphite and paper grounds with a single wine accent. The signature move: headline text inks in with color as it scrolls into view. Asymmetric, alternating, unmistakably digital.

01 — Color

Palette

A near-black graphite and a cool, crisp paper form the base — not the warm bone/cream of the earlier systems. One accent, a deep wine, carries every signal. No gold anywhere.

No gold, anywhere. The client flagged gold-on-bone-and-dark as reading like luxury jewelry rather than wealth management — this system avoids metallic accents entirely. Sections alternate ink and paper grounds; this system is not "mostly dark" or "mostly light."

--t-smArchivo · 400

We hold a deliberately small client list so every relationship sits with a partner, not a desk. Every number on this page updates the way our reporting does.

Rules

  • Headlines: Archivo, sentence case, weight 600–700, tight tracking (-.03em). Never uppercase, never a terminal period.
  • Labels: Space Mono, uppercase, wide tracking, muted stone — small metadata only (dates, roles, counters), never a headline or link.
  • Body: Archivo 400–500, sentence case, softened tier. strong lifts to full ink/paper at weight 600.
  • No serif anywhere — not even reserved for figures. This is the one non-negotiable carried over from client feedback.

03 — The signature move

Scroll-ink headlines

Scroll this section slowly. Each word below starts muted and inks in to wine as it crosses into view — built independently for this system, not copied from any specific reference site.

Capital that responds as fast as the decisions behind it.

Applied to hero and section headlines only — not body copy, or the effect loses its meaning through repetition. Disabled under prefers-reduced-motion; renders fully in wine with JS off.

House marks

The notation here is kinetic, not printed — the opposite move from a ledger system.

Motion

  • Scroll-ink headline: the signature — phrases shift --stone → --wine, staggered per word, as they cross into view.
  • Progress rule: fixed top bar, width tied 1:1 to scroll position.
  • Counting numerals: stats count up from 0 on first scroll-into-view.
  • Split-scroll pin: sticky panels for The Firm / Approach sections.
  • All motion gated behind a .js class; scroll-ink and counters render in final state under prefers-reduced-motion.

09 — Don'ts & constraints

The guardrails

Brand / visual

  • No gold, anywhere — reads as jewelry, not wealth management.
  • No serif — not even reserved for figures.
  • Never a symmetric, centered "typical website" layout — asymmetry is load-bearing here.
  • Never reuse System I's bone/charcoal palette or System II's ruled-ledger notation.
  • Never clay and wine at equal weight — wine leads, clay is a rare single touch.

Technical (carry into the Wix build)

  • Self-contained — one HTML file per page, CSS/JS inline. No external JS libraries.
  • Google Fonts is the only external runtime resource.
  • Scroll effects must degrade gracefully with JS off and respect reduced-motion.
  • Mobile-first, smooth on low-end laptops.
